  • AISKRIM COOKIES



    • Ingredient

    - 1 Buttercup (250g)
    - 1 cup of castor sugar
    - 1 Cup Flour Self
    - 3 Cups of Wheat Flour
    - 1 Cup of Corn Flour
    - 1/2 teaspoon Baking Powder
    - 1 teaspoon Vanilla Ice Tea
    - 1 egg
    - Compound Chocolate
    - Chocolate / Rainbow Rice

    How to Cook

    1. Beat butter with icing sugar until fluffy.
    2. Add egg yolk and vanilla essence - beat until well combined.
    3. Add flour, corn flour and almond powder - mix until soft.
    4. Mix batter until about 1cm thick.
    5. Apply it to an ice cream shape.
    6. Bake the dough at a 180 degree Celsius heat for 30 minutes.
    7. Then cool the cookies before dipping them in melted chocolate.
    8. Sprinkle the decorations. Example: rainbow rice beads



  • HONEY CORNFLAKE



    • Ingredient

    - 5 cups of Cornflakes
    - 1/4 cup honey
    - 1/4 cup butter
    - 1 1/2 tbsp of caster sugar
    - Cherry or bead distribution, for decoration
    - paper cup

    How to Cook

    1. Melt butter in a small saucepan over low heat and add honey and sugar, stirring thoroughly.
    2. In a bowl, whisk together cornflakes so that they are easy to put in the cake.
    3. Pour melted butter mixture into bowl of cornflakes, stirring until conflakes are coated properly.
    4. Place into a paper cup, garnish with cherries or sprinkle with beads.
    5. Bake for 10-15 minutes, at 150 degrees celsius.



  • POPIA CHEESE



    • Ingredient

    - 1 pack of popia
    - Cheese powder
    - Chili powder
    - Sugar

    How to Cook

    1. Scissor popia into square
    2. Mix 4 tablespoons cheese powder, 2 tablespoons sugar and 1 tablespoon chili powder
    3. Fried the popia until golden and toss
    4. In a sealed container, put popia in the container, sprinkle the powdered mix and shake it evenly.
    5. Ready to eat or for Hari Raya



  • COCONUT COOKIES



    • Ingredient

    - 1 3/4 cup sweet potato flour
    - 1/4 cup flour flour
    - 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
    - 1/4 cup caster sugar
    - 1/2 cup (130 grams) butter
    - 1 cup white grated coconut
    - 1/2 cup of vanilla essence
    - 1 egg yolk grade A
    - Salt pinch (when using salted butter, no salt required)
    - 1 egg yolk, to coat on cookies

    How to Cook

    1. Grated oil without oil in medium heat until golden brown, but make sure it does not become as greasy as silver.
    Stir constantly to keep from burning. Drain and let cool.
    Combine sweet potato flour, wheat flour and baking powder, mix well and sift. Dismiss.
    2. Turn butter and sugar to pale, add egg yolk and vanilla essence, turning well. Meanwhile, mix the grated coconut oil without the sifted flour, and add it to the butter mixture until smooth and the dough becomes a dough.
    3. Divide the dough into 2 portions, cover the remaining dough with a clean kitchen towel. Can dough between 2 layers of clean plastic about 1/2 cm thick. Canai carefully crescent shaped mold and arrange in a baking tray. Make it ready. Wipe the cookie surface with egg yolk.
    4. Bake in a preheated oven at 160C (depending on the oven) for 25 minutes or until golden yellow. Take out the cookies and let cool in the tray. Transfer and store in airtight containers. How much or little biscuits will we have can depend on the mold we use. Good luck!
